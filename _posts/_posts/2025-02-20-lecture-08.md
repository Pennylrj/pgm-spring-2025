## Introduction
This lecture covers parameter learning in fully observed Bayesian networks, focusing on Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE) and Bayesian parameter estimation.

## Learning in Graphical Models
**Goal:** Given a set of independent samples (assignments to random variables), find the best Bayesian Network (both DAG and CPDs).

- **Structure Learning:** Discover the best graph structure that represents the dependencies among variables.
- **Parameter Learning:** Given a fixed structure, estimate the parameters of the conditional probability distributions (CPDs).

## Parameter Estimation for Fully-Observed BNs
- **Data:** \( D = (x_1, x_2, x_3, \dots, x_N) \)
- Assume the graph \( G \) is known and fixed.
- **Goal:** Estimate parameters from a dataset of \( N \) independent, identically distributed (iid) training examples \( D \).
- Model should be completely observable: no missing values, no hidden variables.

$$
\ell(\theta; D) = \log P(D \mid \theta) = \log \prod_{n} \prod_{i} P(x_{n,i} \mid x_{n,\pi_i}, \theta_i)
$$

$$
= \sum_{i} \sum_{n} \log P(x_{n,i} \mid x_{n,\pi_i}, \theta_i)
$$

## Discrete Distributions
### Bernoulli Distribution
$$
P(x) = p^x (1 - p)^{1 - x}
$$

### Multinomial Distribution
$$
X = [X_1, X_2, X_3, X_4, X_5, X_6], \quad X_j \in \{0,1\}, \quad \sum_{j=1}^{6} X_j = 1
$$

$$
P(X_j = 1) = \theta_j, \quad \sum_{j=1}^{6} \theta_j = 1
$$

## Example: Multinomial Model
- Data: We observed \( N \) iid die rolls (K-sided): \( D = \{5, 1, K, \dots, 3\} \).
- Likelihood function:

$$
P(x_1, x_2, \dots, x_N \mid \theta) = \prod_{n=1}^{N} P(x_n \mid \theta) = \prod_{k} \theta_k^{n_k}
$$

## MLE: Constrained Optimization
To find \( \theta \), we maximize:

$$
l(\theta; D) = \sum_k n_k \log \theta_k
$$

Subject to the constraint:

$$
\sum_{k} \theta_k = 1
$$

Using **Lagrange multipliers**, we solve:

$$
\hat{\theta}_{k,MLE} = \frac{1}{N} \sum_n x_{n,k}
$$

## Bayesian Estimation
Using the **Dirichlet prior**:

$$
P(\theta) = \frac{\Gamma(\sum_k \alpha_k)}{\prod_k \Gamma(\alpha_k)} \prod_k \theta_k^{\alpha_k - 1}
$$

Posterior mean estimation:

$$
\theta_k = \frac{n_k + \alpha_k}{N + |\alpha|}
$$

## Key Takeaways
- **MLE and Bayesian Estimation** are core methods in parameter learning.
- **Decomposability** simplifies learning in fully observed BNs.
- **Dirichlet prior** is used for discrete Bayesian Networks, **Normal prior** for Gaussian models.
